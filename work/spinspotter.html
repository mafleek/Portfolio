SpinSpotter

Detecting framing bias at the phrase level, with explainable AI that teaches as it flags.

View Prototype Download Paper (PDF) Slide Deck (Canva)
Role
UX + strategy + research synthesis (team project)
Team: Powerpuffs • Course project
Why this exists

Most tools rate bias at the outlet level. SpinSpotter targets the quieter problem: phrasing that nudges interpretation inside an otherwise “accurate” article.

Core interactions
  • Highlight spin devices (loaded language, hyperbole, selective framing)
  • Explain why a phrase was flagged (XAI tooltip)
  • Suggest neutral rewrites
  • Compare coverage across sources
  • Learning module + accessibility controls
What to look at

If you only have 60 seconds: open the prototype and click an article highlight. The “why” is the product.

Phase 1A Lo-fi concepts Final paper
Next iterations
  • Integrate a preliminary NLP classifier (beyond mock results)
  • Run larger-scale usability study with diverse demographics
  • Turn the experience into a real browser extension workflow
Recruiter-friendly summary
Problem → gap → concept → prototype → evaluation loop

SpinSpotter reframes “media bias tools” as a learning experience: not just labeling sources, but teaching users how bias is written. The interface prioritizes transparency, control, and accessibility, so the AI feels like a lens, not a verdict.
